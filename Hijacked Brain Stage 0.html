What Actually Happened To Your Brain
The Loop

Dopamine isn't the pleasure chemical.
It's the anticipation chemical.

It spikes before the reward, not during.
Every time you pull down the refresh lever, your brain releases a small hit.

The reward is unpredictable.
That's the trick.

Unpredictable rewards are more addictive than reliable ones.
This is the same mechanism that makes slot machines work.

Your phone is an emotional slot machine you carry in your pocket.
Can you see the similarity?

It rolls down in the hope of the jackpot.
The prize is your attention.

It's costing you a lot more than money.
And the bill is coming soon.

But you scroll again to forget that too.

After enough hits, the brain adapts.
The receptors get less sensitive.

The same input produces less feeling.
You need more.

More scrolling, more videos, more refreshing.
The high gets smaller.
The dose gets larger.

This is tolerance.
It's the same brain pattern as any other addiction, just with a socially acceptable substance.

The result you feel every day?
Ordinary life becomes flat.

Reading feels boring.
A walk in nature feels boring.
A conversation with a real person feels boring within the first minute.

Nothing is wrong with reality.
Your baseline is broken.

You've calibrated your nervous system to the most engineered stimulus in human history.
Of course everything else feels grey.
